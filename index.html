Tor node

A Tor network node, also known as a relay, plays a crucial role in the functionality of the Tor network. These nodes act as intermediaries that help anonymize and encrypt internet traffic by passing it through multiple layers of relays before reaching its destination. There are three types of Tor nodes: entry nodes, middle nodes, and exit nodes. Entry nodes receive user traffic and pass it to middle nodes, which further anonymize and route it to exit nodes. Exit nodes then send the traffic to its final destination on the internet. By participating in this relay process, Tor nodes contribute to the network's overall goal of providing users with privacy and anonymity online.
This node is a middle relay, which means it helps route traffic between entry and exit nodes. Middle relays are essential for maintaining the speed and efficiency of the Tor network, as they handle a significant portion of the traffic flow while also providing an additional layer of anonymity for users. By running a middle relay, this node helps support the Tor network's mission of promoting online privacy and freedom of speech.
